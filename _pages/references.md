The list below covers resources that I found useful and referenced many times while pursuing my M.Sc. in Cybersecurity.

## Major Industry Websites
### [Center for Internet Security](https://www.cisecurity.org/cis-benchmarks/)
The Center for Internet Security (CIS) offers guidance, templates, and leading practices to help improve organizations’ security postures. CIS is known for its “benchmarks,” a set of community-driven guides to hardening systems based on many vendor products.

### [CSO](https://www.csoonline.com/)
CSO (also known as CSO Online) offers content tailored to cybersecurity leaders, covering a range of topics such as risk management, network security, threats, and cybercrime.

### [Dark Reading](https://www.darkreading.com/)
Dark Reading is a popular new site and online community for security professionals, offering insights from leading security researchers and CISOs. It provides an independent view on recent attacks, threat intelligence, cloud security, vulnerabilities, and others.

### [Internet Storm Center](https://isc.sans.edu/)
The Internet Storm Center (ISC) is a free service maintained by the SANS Institute that monitors malicious activity on the Internet by gathering data from millions of intrusion detection sensors. The ISC analysts frequently share insights on how threats operate with detailed procedures to reverse engineer and analyze artifacts.

### [SecureList by Kaspersky](https://securelist.com/)
SecureList is a blog with Kaspersky's latest threat intelligence reports and APT analysis. It is a valuable source of information providing details on how threats operate and how to detect them. SecureList is not restricted to the U. S.; it offers insights into malicious actors from many parts of the world.

### [OWASP Foundation](https://owasp.org/)
The Open Web Application Security Project (OWASP) has served the cybersecurity community for almost two decades, providing tools, training, and community-driven practices to secure software. OWASP is well known for their [OWASP Top 10](https://owasp.org/www-project-top-ten/) project, a list maintained to raise awareness of common web application security risks.

### [SANS Reading Room](https://www.sans.org/white-papers/)
The SANS Reading Room hosts a plethora of peer-reviewed cybersecurity papers covering a range of cybersecurity topics, from cloud security to security awareness.    

## Government Resources
### [Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA)](https://www.cisa.gov/)
The CISA is the leading agency in understanding and handling the U.S.’s cyber and physical infrastructure risk, coordinating efforts across government and industry partners towards a resilient and secure critical infrastructure. The website offers guidance on handling cybersecurity incidents and information on the latest threats.

### [National Vulnerability Database](https://nvd.nist.gov/)
The NVD is a repository of vulnerability data covering many products and vendors. It includes details about vulnerabilities such as description, risk ratings, and references to advisories with recommendations to remediate the issue.

### [NIST Cybersecurity](https://www.nist.gov/cybersecurity)
The NIST Cybersecurity website links many of NIST's standards, guidelines, best practices, and other resources. It is the home of the [Cybersecurity Framework](https://www.nist.gov/cyberframework), a widely adopted reference to build cybersecurity services.

## Blogs and Opinions
### [Daniel Miessler](https://danielmiessler.com/)
Daniel Miessler is a prolific cybersecurity researcher. His posts covered some of the basic topics in the industry (such as the difference between Penetration Testing and Vulnerability Assessment) to philosophical discussions surrounding the future of privacy.

### [Errata Security](https://blog.erratasec.com/)
Robert Graham is the creator of BlackICE and masscan, two widely used cybersecurity tools. He and David Maynor use the blog to post their personal views and observations on the latest cybersecurity topics.  

### [Google Security Blog](https://security.googleblog.com/)
The Google Security Blog covers highly technical topics, including the latest updates and insights from Google. Those interested in exploit research might feel more included to visit the [Google's Project Zero blog](https://googleprojectzero.blogspot.com/).

### [Krebs on Security](https://krebsonsecurity.com/)
Brian Krebs is one of the most reliable sources of cybersecurity news on the Internet. A former reporter for _The Washington Post_, Krebs gained notoriety due to his in-depth cybercrime coverage. His daily posts cover latest threats and malicious activities.

### [Schneier on Security](https://www.schneier.com/)
Bruce Schneier is a renowned cybersecurity leader, author of many cybersecurity books and well know for his mailing list “Crypto-Gram. Schneier specialty is cryptography, but his posts cover personal takes on many cybersecurity aspects.

## Tools and Repositories
### [Burp Suite](https://portswigger.net/burp)
Burp Suite combines vulnerability scanner and interception proxy to help cybersecurity professionals identify vulnerabilities in web applications.  Burp is available for free with limited functionalities or in its professional version, which allows the user to extend its functionalities using Python scripts.

### [Daniel Miessler’s SecList](https://github.com/danielmiessler/SecLists)
A repository maintained by Daniel Miessler with a collection of resources to help security testers. Includes wordlists for password attacks, web shells, and payloads.

### [Kali Linux](https://www.kali.org/)
Kali Linux is a Debian-based Linux distribution created to help penetration testers perform security assessments. It comes with many tools pre-installed, including network scanners and exploitation frameworks.  

### [Metasploit](https://www.metasploit.com/)
Metasploit is a penetration testing framework that allows the user to write and share exploits, payloads, and scanners. The open-source version is actively maintained a widely used in the industry.  

### [Nmap](https://nmap.org/)
Nmap is an open-source network scanning tool widely used in the cybersecurity industry. Since its inception in 1997, Nmap evolved to become faster and extendable through its scripting engine, becoming an essential tool in the penetration tester’s toolkit.

### [Semgrep](https://semgrep.dev/)
Semgrep is a static analysis tool widely used to perform code reviews. Semgrep leverages community-created rules to identify issues affecting code written in many languages, including Java, C, Python, Go, and Ruby.

### [WebGoat](https://owasp.org/www-project-webgoat/)
Webgoat is an insecure web application designed to raise awareness of common vulnerabilities. Aside from training ground, it can also serve as a test target to benchmark tools.

### [Wireshark](https://www.wireshark.org/)
Wireshark is a popular network protocol analyzer that helps cybersecurity professionals to monitor traffic, inspect its contents to identify anomalies and vulnerabilities.

### [Zabbix](https://www.zabbix.com/)
Zabbix is an open-source network and application monitoring solution that allows users to keep track of many metrics in almost real-time. Zabbix is extensible through its API, providing users with the ability to automate tasks and integrate third-party software.

## Certification and Training
Coursera published a [relevant article about Cybersecurity certifications](https://www.coursera.org/articles/popular-cybersecurity-certifications) that lists primary options for many career paths and the associated investment. The following is a non-exhaustive list of major training and certification providers:

### [(ISC)<sup>2</sup>](https://www.isc2.org)
The (ISC)<sup>2</sup> offers many certifications geared towards cybersecurity leaders, such as the Certified Information Systems Security Professional. The CISSP is widely accepted and recognized.

### [CompTIA](https://www.comptia.org)
CompTIA provides several technical IT certifications. The Security+ is globally recognized as an entry-level certification covering the foundations of cybersecurity.

### [EC-Council](https://www.eccouncil.org/)
The EC-Council became well known for its Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH) certification. Since then, the company expanded its offering to include programs covering a range of cybersecurity domains.

### [ISACA](https://www.isaca.org)
The Information Systems Audit and Control Association (ISACA) offers training, certifications, and methodologies to support audit and IT controls activities. Cybersecurity professionals might benefit from their Certified Information Security Manager (CISM) and Certified Data Privacy Solutions Engineer (CDPS) certifications.

### [Offensive Security](https://www.offensive-security.com/)
Offensive Security (OffSec) offers training and certification focused on penetration testing. OffSec is the maintainer of the widely-used Kali Linux and the Offensive Security Certified Professional (OSCP) certification.

### [SANS Institute](https://www.sans.org)
SANS is a leader in providing cybersecurity training and offers certifications widely accepted by the cybersecurity industry. SANS is also the maintainer of many initiatives that help advance the community, such as the reading room and the Internet Storm Center.
